Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) About Health Data Sharing

Learn About Secure Health Data Sharing

Health data sharing refers to the process of exchanging a patient's health information among healthcare providers, insurance companies, and other relevant entities involved in patient care.

Secure health data sharing ensures that sensitive patient information is kept confidential and prevents unauthorized access, reducing the risk of identity theft, fraud, and breaches.

Health data typically includes medical history, test results, prescriptions, diagnoses, treatment plans, insurance details, and medical images.

When health data is shared securely, it improves care coordination, reduces medical errors, helps prevent duplicate tests, and ensures timely and accurate treatments.

Mishandling health data can lead to breaches of privacy, identity theft, incorrect treatments, or unauthorized access to sensitive information.
